## Market Analysis

### Target Audience Deep Dive

**Primary Segment: JAMB Candidates (18-22 years)**
- **Psychographics:**
  - Anxious about admission outcomes
  - Overwhelmed by information fragmentation
  - Seeking guidance and validation
  - Mobile-first digital natives
  - Price-sensitive but willing to pay for value
  - Influenced by peer recommendations

- **Pain Points:**
  - Fear of missing application deadlines
  - Uncertainty about cutoff scores and eligibility
  - Lack of trusted guidance
  - Hidden costs and surprises
  - Information overload from multiple sources

- **Aspirations:**
  - Get admitted to a reputable institution
  - Study a program that leads to good jobs
  - Make parents proud
  - Avoid costly mistakes
  - Feel confident in their choices

**Secondary Segment: Parents/Guardians (35-55 years)**
- Concerned about ROI on education investment
- Want assurance of institutional quality
- Need transparency on total costs
- Seeking validation of decisions
- Less tech-savvy, need simplicity

**Tertiary Segment: School Counselors (30-50 years)**
- Need efficient tools for mass guidance
- Want updated information
- Require credibility and authority
- Need export/sharing capabilities

### Competitive Landscape

**Current State:**
- No dominant player in Nigerian education data
- Fragmented information across institutional websites
- JAMB provides only exam services, not guidance
- Education consultancies are expensive (â‚¦50k-â‚¦200k)
- Word-of-mouth and seniors are primary sources

**Opportunity:**
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** - Create a new category: "Educational Decision Platform"
- Not competing with JAMB (exam body) or universities (providers)
- Competing with: ignorance, misinformation, expensive consultants

**Threat Analysis:**
- JAMB could launch competing service (low likelihood - not their core business)
- Well-funded edtech startups could pivot into this space
- Universities could band together to create official platform

**Secondary Color: Achievement Green**
- Hex: #16A34A (Fresh Green)

- Psychology: Success, growth, progress, positivity
- Usage: Success states, CTAs, highlights

**Accent Color: Energy Orange**
- Hex: #EA580C (Warm Orange)
- RGB: 234, 88, 12
- Psychology: Urgency, energy, action, excitement
- Usage: Alerts, deadlines, important actions

**Neutral Palette:**
- Dark: #1E293B (Slate)
- Gray: #64748B (Cool Gray)
- Light: #F1F5F9 (Off-White)
- White: #FFFFFF

**Why These Colors:**
- Blue: Aligns with education, trust, Nigerian flag
- Green: Nigerian flag colors, success/growth symbolism
- Orange: Urgency without panic, stands out in market
- Avoids: Red (too aggressive), Purple (too playful), Yellow (poor readability)

**Typography:**

**Headings: Inter (Bold/SemiBold)**
- Modern, legible, professional
- Google Font (free, web-optimized)

**Body: Inter (Regular/Medium)**
- Same family for consistency
- Excellent readability on screens

**Display (Hero): Poppins (Bold)**
- Friendly, energetic for landing pages
- Pairs well with Inter

**Code/Data: JetBrains Mono**
- For technical elements, data displays

**Imagery Style:**
- **Photography:** Real Nigerian students (diverse, authentic, aspirational)
- **Illustrations:** Modern, flat, diverse skin tones, optimistic scenarios
- **Icons:** Minimalist, consistent stroke width, functional
- **Avoid:** Stock photos of non-Nigerian students, cheesy graduation photos

**Design Principles:**
1. **Clarity First:** Every design choice enhances understanding
2. **Mobile-First:** 80% of users on mobile - optimize for small screens
3. **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (color contrast, font size)
4. **Consistency:** Design system with reusable components
5. **Speed:** Optimize assets for Nigerian internet speeds

## Brand Guidelines

### Logo Usage

**Do's:**
- âœ… Use official logo files only
- âœ… Maintain clear space (minimum 20px around logo)

- âœ… Scale proportionally

**Don'ts:**
- âŒ Stretch or distort logo

### Tone of Voice Examples

**Helpful (Not Condescending):**
- âœ… "Here's what you need to know about JAMB cutoff scores"
- âŒ "Obviously, you should check the cutoff first"

**Confident (Not Arrogant):**
- âœ… "We've helped 10,000+ students get admitted"
- âŒ "We're the best and everyone else is wrong"

**Encouraging (Not Dismissive):**
- âœ… "Getting admitted is challenging, but we're here to help"
- âŒ "Don't worry, it's easy!"

**Clear (Not Jargon-Heavy):**
- âœ… "Programs that accept your UTME score"
- âŒ "Eligibility-filtered program aggregation"

### Writing Guidelines

**Headlines:**
- Use active voice: "Find Your Program" not "Programs Can Be Found"
- Lead with benefit: "Save 10 Hours of Research" not "Comprehensive Database"
- Keep under 10 words for clarity

**Body Copy:**
- Short sentences (15-20 words max)
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences)
- Bullet points for lists
- Clear call-to-action

**Error Messages:**
- Explain what happened: "We couldn't find programs matching your search"
- Suggest next steps: "Try adjusting your filters or browse all programs"
- Be empathetic: "Sorry about that" not "Error 404"

```css
Spinner with brand colors

/* Page Transitions */
Fade in: 300ms
Slide in: 400ms
Scale up: 250ms

/* Scroll Animations */
Use Intersection Observer
Fade in when 20% visible
Stagger children by 50ms
```

**Loading States:**
```bash
1. Skeleton Loaders (use for content)
   - Program cards: Pulse animated rectangles
   - Lists: Repeated line placeholders
   - Images: Gray background with shimmer

2. Spinners (use for actions)
   - Button loading: Inline spinner
   - Page loading: Centered brand spinner
   - Infinite scroll: Bottom spinner

3. Progress Indicators
   - Multi-step forms: Step indicator
   - File uploads: Progress bar
   - Long operations: Percentage + time estimate
```
